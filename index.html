Rick Lin

I'm a Certified Electrical Engineering Technologist with a strong focus on building electrical system design. Over the years, I’ve worked across a wide variety types of projects—contributing to safe, efficient, and code-compliant electrical systems. Whether it's power distribution, lighting, fire alarm, or infrastructure support, I approach every project with attention to detail and a commitment to collaboration. Beyond design, I’m deeply involved in construction administration and BIM/Revit model optimization, helping ensure ideas on paper become reliable, buildable systems on-site. My goal is to deliver technically sound, coordinated designs that serve both the client’s goals and the realities of construction. I work well independently or as part of a multidisciplinary team, and I’m always seeking to simplify complexity through thoughtful planning and streamlined documentation.

Area of Expertise

I specialize in the design and coordination of power distribution systems for a wide variety types of buildings. My work emphasizes efficiency, reliability, and code compliance—from service entrance and distribution panels to branch circuits and emergency power systems. I take pride in delivering robust electrical layouts that meet client needs while integrating seamlessly with architectural and mechanical systems.

Thoughtful lighting design is critical to both form and function. I develop tailored lighting systems that enhance occupant experience, meet energy efficiency goals, and comply with regional codes and standards. My expertise includes photometric analysis, fixture specification, and the integration of lighting control systems—whether simple occupancy sensing or complex networked DALI/0-10V controls for modern smart buildings.

Protecting lives and property is at the core of every fire alarm system I design. My approach includes strategic device layout and adherence to local building and fire codes (e.g., NFPA 72, ULC-S524). Whether working on new construction or retrofit projects, I ensure systems are well-integrated with other building services, easy to maintain, and inspection-ready.

While I don’t design low voltage systems themselves, I provide the essential power and pathway infrastructure they depend on. From structured cabling raceways to telecom room power provisions, my designs enable smooth deployment of AV, security, data, and communications systems. I coordinate closely with IT and specialty vendors to ensure compatibility and future expandability.

A great design only succeeds when it’s built as intended. I actively support projects through the construction phase—reviewing shop drawings, answering RFIs, and performing field reviews to ensure electrical installations align with design documents. My goal is to be a responsive and solutions-oriented partner throughout construction, helping resolve issues quickly and clearly.

I use BIM not just as a modeling tool, but as a platform for coordination and efficiency. With a strong foundation in Revit, I build clean, lightweight, and highly-coordinated electrical models that support clash detection, quantity takeoffs, and clear documentation. I also develop and implement workflows that streamline model maintenance and ensure consistency across large, multi-phase projects.

Division

Commercial Buildings In the commercial sector, I’ve contributed to office towers, retail spaces, restaurants, and mixed-use developments. These projects demand flexible power distribution, efficient lighting, and reliable fire alarm systems—all delivered under tight timelines and coordinated across disciplines. I focus on designs that are scalable, tenant-friendly, and energy-conscious, ensuring long-term performance and minimal disruption during future fit-outs.

Laboratories & Research Facilities Laboratory environments require highly specialized electrical designs that support strict operational standards, redundancy, and user safety. I’ve worked on labs with high-power equipment loads, controlled lighting environments, and critical backup power needs. I collaborate closely with lab planners and mechanical engineers to ensure electrical systems support lab operations, meet clean power requirements, and adhere to safety codes and equipment specs.

Institutional Buildings I have extensive experience supporting the design of schools, colleges, and healthcare-related institutional facilities. These projects often require enhanced lighting, robust fire alarm systems, and careful coordination with low-voltage and AV systems. My role is to ensure the electrical infrastructure supports a safe, functional, and inclusive environment for students, staff, and visitors.

Federal & Government Facilities Working on government and federal buildings requires attention to heightened design standards, security protocols, and documentation rigor. I’ve contributed to civic projects and infrastructure upgrades involving secure power distribution, code-compliant fire alarm layouts, and clean, standards-based Revit models. I understand the importance of transparency, traceability, and collaboration when working on publicly funded work.

Public & Community Spaces Transit stations, and other public-use buildings present a unique set of design priorities—ranging from accessibility and safety to long-term durability and user experience. I’ve delivered electrical designs for these spaces that prioritize public comfort while meeting strict municipal requirements and sustainability goals.

Residential Projects My residential experience spans everything from low-rise multi-unit developments to multi high-rise buildings complex. I provide tailored designs that balance code compliance, homeowner preferences, and construction realities. This includes lighting layouts, panel configurations, and low-voltage conduit infrastructure to support modern smart home features. I focus on practical, future-ready designs that support both everyday living and high-performance systems.

Healthcare Facilities Electrical systems in healthcare environments must meet the highest standards of reliability, safety, and redundancy. I’ve worked on clinics, hospitals, and specialized medical facilities where emergency power systems, life-safety circuits, and equipment zoning are critical. My designs support uninterrupted operation of essential systems, meet CSA/Z32 or equivalent standards, and prioritize clean coordination with mechanical and architectural teams.

Light Industrial Buildings In light industrial spaces like warehouses and manufacturing units, electrical design focuses on durability, flexibility, and load handling. I provide power distribution layouts that account for large equipment, lighting for high-bay environments, and robust infrastructure for future expansion. I also pay close attention to equipment clearances, grounding, and coordination with specialty systems like compressed air or process utilities.
